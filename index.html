Progress Report No. 1 — The Lab

A small lab, run like a lab.

Algernon Laboratories is a three-person company building behavioral intelligence systems for markets that incumbent compliance and credit infrastructure cannot profitably serve. We are bootstrapped, AI-leveraged, and deliberately small — by design, not by circumstance.

progris riport — algernon labs, 2026

The name is a deliberate borrow from Daniel Keyes. Algernon is the lab mouse who runs the maze first — who learns the path so the next runner can follow. That posture is the company's operating thesis: run the experiment, publish the result, let the next person move faster.

We treat every product as a falsifiable hypothesis with an explicit kill criterion. We ship narrow surfaces that work, instead of broad surfaces that almost work. We use AI as engineering leverage — not as marketing — and we measure ourselves against revenue, not against the thickness of our roadmap.

Progress Report No. 3 — Operating Model

How a three-person lab compresses a ten-person roadmap

Four constraints we design around. Together they explain why we ship the things we ship and refuse the things we refuse.

/ 01

Bootstrap-first

No VC. No runway pressure to spend. A profitable small business is the goal — outside capital is an option we evaluate from a position of strength, not a milestone we chase.

/ 02

AI as leverage

We use AI aggressively to compress engineering, research, and documentation. We do not confuse "AI builds it faster" with "AI builds the business faster." Customer conversations still take calendar time.

/ 03

Profitable before scale

We build to be profitable at small scale first. Growth comes from proven economics, not from raised capital. The maze gets walked before it gets paved.

/ 04

Falsifiability over conviction

Every phase has a kill criterion. Thesis falsified → we pivot or shut down the phase. The point is to learn fast, not to be right. We publish the results either way.
