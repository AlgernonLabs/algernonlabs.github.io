Field Report No. 1 — Thesis

Wrong calibration is worse than no signal.

Chainalysis, TRM, and Elliptic built their models on Western exchange data. That ground truth is wrong for this market. A Lagos P2P off-ramp looks like laundering. Field Report No. 3 — Calibration

How signals are built

Every score reflects a sequence of decisions about what counts as normal. We make those decisions visible — and we calibrate them to local context, not to imported defaults.

I

Index the Chain

Celo first. Full transaction graph indexed into Apache AGE. BSC and Tron follow customer demand, not speculation. The indexer is a cost center to customers and a strategic asset to us — both true.

II

Build the Behavioral Graph

Counterparties, timing distributions, amount clustering, agent topology. Raw on-chain motion becomes structured behavioral patterns that a model can reason about.

III

Detect Local Patterns

Chama rotations. P2P off-ramp clusters around agent networks. MiniPay phone-number families. Airtime → stablecoin → cash triangles. Patterns that look anomalous to generic models — and aren't.

IV

Score With Explanations

Every flag carries its reasoning. Compliance teams need to defend their decisions to regulators, partners, and their own users. Black-box scores don't survive that conversation.

Field Report No. 5 — Timeline

The phases

Every phase has a decision gate. If the gate fails, we pivot or shut down — we don't slow-play. Falsifiability over conviction.

Phase 0 — Foundation

Demoable API

Indexer running on Celo mainnet. Trust scoring layer built. 14-day demoable sprint to first externally-callable Shield endpoint.

No demoable API by end of Q2 → re-scope.
Phase 1 — Beachhead

First Paying Customers

Shield API live. Three to five design partners (Cashramp-class operators in Kenya, Nigeria, Ghana). Pricing validated against the sub-$2K/month tier.

Fewer than 3 paying customers in 6 months → buyer doesn't exist at stated urgency.
Phase 2 — Validation

Calibration Benchmark

Published precision/recall against generic models on labeled African flow data. The calibration thesis becomes evidence — or it dies here, honestly.

No measurable lift over generic models → reposition on price + scope alone.
Phase 3 — Scale

Network Compounds

Multi-chain coverage where customers need it. Compliance Dashboard ships. Regulator-track features (informal OTC broker detection) open the second buyer conversation.

Acquisition CAC stays high after 20 customers → niche may not generalize.
